Step 1 of 6
Who are you and what do you do?
Your background, expertise, and what makes you credible. AI needs to know who is talking.
Example: "I'm a UGC creator and content strategist with 3 years of experience. I specialize in beauty and skincare brands. I've worked with 40+ brands including Glossier and The Ordinary."
Step 2 of 6
What does your brand voice sound like?
Describe how you write and talk. Direct? Casual? Professional? Funny? Give 2-3 examples of sentences that sound like you.
Example: "Direct and conversational. I swear occasionally. I use short sentences. I never say 'leverage' or 'synergy.' I sound like I'm talking to a friend who happens to know business. Example: 'That pitch template is trash and I'm going to fix it for you.'"
Step 3 of 6
Who is your audience?
Describe them in their own words. What do they struggle with? What keeps them up at night? How do they talk about their problems?
Example: "Female UGC creators, 25-34, making $500-$3,000/month. They get brand deals but always feel underpaid. They say things like 'I never know what to charge' and 'brands always try to lowball me.' They want financial security from their creative work."
Step 4 of 6
What do you sell or offer?
Your products, services, prices, and what makes them different. Include results your customers get.
Example: "I sell the UGC Closer Kit ($497) - a digital product with rate calculators, negotiation scripts, and a 175-brand database. Customers typically increase their brand deal rates by $200-$600 per deal within the first week."
Step 5 of 6
What platforms do you create content for?
List your platforms and any specific rules or preferences for each. Do you avoid certain formats? Prefer certain lengths?
Example: "Instagram (carousels and Reels, educational focus), TikTok (talking head 30-60 sec), X (short-form text, no links in post body, data-driven), Threads (longer reflections, personal voice), YouTube (tutorials 10-20 min)."
Step 6 of 6
What should AI never do when writing for you?
Your non-negotiable rules. Words to avoid. Tones to avoid. Things that would make content sound unlike you.
Example: "Never use em dashes. Never say 'leverage,' 'unlock,' or 'game-changer.' Never use emojis in long-form content. Never write in a corporate voice. Never suggest coaching or group programs as part of my business model. Always sound like a real person, not a brand."
